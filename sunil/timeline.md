**Sunil Abraham** (born 17 June 1973) is an Indian technologist, researcher and public policy leader known for his work on open technologies, digital rights, and Internet governance. 
 
This page presents a timeline biography — a strictly chronological record of key life events and milestones.

## <span id="early-life"></span>Early Life and Education

<div class="timeline">

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">Sunday, 17 June 1973</div>
  <h3>Birth</h3>
  <p>Born in India to A. M. A. Ayrookuzhiel and Ponnamma Abraham. His upbringing in a family rooted in education and theology shaped his social and intellectual curiosity.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1989</div>
  <h3>Completed schooling</h3>
  <p>Completed studies at Clarence High School, Bangalore, where early exposure to community service informed his later civic focus.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1993</div>
  <h3>Founded Students for Peace</h3>
  <p>As a student, established <em>Students for Peace</em>, a non-partisan platform promoting dialogue and non-violence after the communal riots. The group drew thousands to a candlelight demonstration on M. G. Road, Bangalore.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1995</div>
  <h3>Graduation</h3>
  <p>Earned a degree in Industrial Engineering and Management from Dayananda Sagar College of Engineering, Bengaluru University.</p>
</div>

</div>

## <span id="career"></span>Civic and Professional Career

<div class="timeline">

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1996</div>
  <h3>Joined Samuha</h3>
  <p>Worked with the NGO Samuha, exploring low-cost and open technologies to assist rural communication and coordination. This experience grounded his lifelong interest in technology for development.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1998</div>
  <h3>Co-founded Mahiti</h3>
  <p>Co-founded <strong>Mahiti</strong>, providing affordable digital tools for NGOs. Served as Director (1998–2004). Mahiti became one of India’s pioneering social enterprises in FOSS for civil society.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1999</div>
  <h3>Ashoka Fellowship</h3>
  <p>Recognised as an Ashoka Fellow for using open technologies to enhance democratic participation in India.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">Thursday, 10 January 2002</div>
  <h3>Marriage</h3>
  <p>Married Esmeralda Davis at Wesley English Church, Bangalore.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">July 2004 – June 2007</div>
  <h3>Manager, International Open Source Network (IOSN)</h3>
  <p>Served with UNDP’s APDIP programme, promoting open-source software, open standards and open content across the Asia–Pacific region.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">2008</div>
  <h3>Founded the Centre for Internet and Society (CIS)</h3>
  <p>Co-founded CIS in Bangalore. Served as Executive Director from May 2008 to November 2019, building a leading research centre on openness, digital rights, and governance.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">2009 – 2016</div>
  <h3>Research, publications, and advocacy</h3>
  <p>Led multiple initiatives on FOSS, privacy, accessibility, and open government data. Published and edited policy guides, reports, and essays widely cited across the sector.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">Tuesday, 27 September 2011 – Friday, 30 September 2011</div>
  <h3>Internet Governance Forum (IGF)</h3>
  <p>Represented India at IGF 2011, participating in global discussions on privacy, openness, and Internet governance.</p>
</div>

</div>

## <span id="academic"></span>Academic Roles and Current Work

<div class="timeline">

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>

  <p>Joined ArtEZ, Arnhem (Netherlands) as an endowed professor (lector) exploring intersections of art, technology, and public policy.</p>

</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">2020 – present</div>
  <h3>Public Policy Director, Meta India</h3>
  <p>Joined Meta (formerly Facebook India) to lead policy on the data economy and emerging technologies, engaging with government, academia and industry.</p>
</div>
