**Sunil Abraham** (born 17 June 1973) is an Indian technologist, researcher and public policy leader known for his work on open technologies, digital rights, and Internet governance.  

This page presents a **timeline biography** — a strictly chronological record of key life events and milestones.

## Early Life and Education

<div class="timeline">

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">Sunday, 17 June 1973</div>
  <h3>Birth</h3>
  <p>Born in India to A. M. A. Ayrookuzhiel and Ponnamma Abraham. His upbringing in a family rooted in education and theology shaped his social and intellectual curiosity.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1989</div>
  <h3>Completed schooling</h3>
  <p>Completed studies at Clarence High School, Bangalore, where early exposure to community service informed his later civic focus.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1993</div>
  <h3>Founded Students for Peace</h3>
  <p>While studying engineering, founded <em>Students for Peace</em>, a platform for inter-community dialogue and non-violence following the 1993 riots.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1995</div>
  <h3>Graduation</h3>
  <p>Graduated in Industrial Engineering and Management from Dayananda Sagar College of Engineering, Bengaluru University.</p>
</div>

</div>

## Civic and Professional Career

<div class="timeline">

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1996</div>
  <h3>Joined Samuha</h3>
  <p>Worked with the NGO Samuha, exploring how low-cost, open technologies could improve rural communication and coordination.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1998</div>
  <h3>Co-founded Mahiti</h3>
  <p>Co-founded <strong>Mahiti</strong>, a social enterprise offering affordable digital solutions to NGOs. Served as Director (1998–2004).</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">1999</div>
  <h3>Ashoka Fellowship</h3>
  <p>Awarded an Ashoka Fellowship for his pioneering use of open technologies in civic participation.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">Thursday, 10 January 2002</div>
  <h3>Marriage</h3>
  <p>Married Esmeralda Davis at Wesley English Church, Bangalore.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">July 2004 – June 2007</div>
  <h3>Manager, International Open Source Network (IOSN)</h3>
  <p>Worked with UNDP’s APDIP programme to promote open-source software and open standards across the Asia–Pacific region.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">2008</div>
  <h3>Co-founded the Centre for Internet and Society (CIS)</h3>
  <p>Established CIS in Bangalore and served as Executive Director (May 2008 – November 2019), leading research in openness, accessibility, and digital governance.</p>
</div>

<div class="timeline-item">
  <div class="timeline-dot"></div>
  <div class="timeline-date">Tuesday, 27 September 2011 – Friday, 30 September 2011</div>
  <h3>Internet Governance Forum (IGF)</h3>
  <p>Participated in the IGF 2011, contributing to international discussions on openness, privacy, and digital inclusion.</p>
</div>

</div>
